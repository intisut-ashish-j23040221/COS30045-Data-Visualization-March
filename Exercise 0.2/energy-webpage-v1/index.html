Why are energy-efficient products important?

In Australia, all televisions are required to display an Energy Rating Label, which uses a star system to help you compare the efficiency of different TV models. The star rating (ranging from 1 to 10) indicates how efficient a TV is compared to other models of a similar screen size. The higher number of stars, the better. Below the stars, you will find the Energy Consumption figure, measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh) per year. This number is calculated based on standardized testing that assumes the TV is used for 10 hours a day and remains in standby for the other 14 hours. By looking at the kWh figure, you can easily estimate your annual running costs by multiplying it by your local electricity tariff.


Choosing an energy-efficient television is important because it offers significant benefits for both you in terms of finance, and the planet. From a financial perspective, a high-efficiency TV can save you hundreds of dollars in electricity bills over its lifespan compared to lower-rated models. As a result, anyone who uses these energy-efficient TVs are able to reduce their household's carbon footprint which in turn helps the environment. Since a large portion of Australia's electricity is still generated from fossil fuels, opting for a TV that uses less power helps reduce greenhouse gas emissions and supports the national transition toward a more sustainable energy future.
